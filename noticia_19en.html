Argentina advances in the prevention and control of money laundering

On June 14, the Argentine Foundation for the Study and Analysis of the Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing (FAPLA)



On June 14, the Argentine Foundation for the Study and Analysis of the Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing (FAPLA), held the 14th annual meeting that was attended by national authorities and prominent local specialists.

Hernán Zavalía Lagos, president of FAPLA, during his opening speech stated “during this year we are seeing a clear order in the control system and an increasingly strong work between the public and private sectors to make more efficient the fight against money laundering in our country ”.

For his part, the Minister of Justice of the Nation, Germán Garavano, highlighted the great call for the event and remarked: "Drug trafficking is one of the greatest exponents of money laundering and its flow of money is the central engine of activity. They are not worried about destroying a drug kiosk or killing a "mule" because they take it as a contingency that is part of the business. The only way to combat it is by detecting the money. That is why we all have to focus on the confiscation of assets, of money, and not so much of people. Today we have an appropriate legal framework to do so ”.

In this sense, the new generation of FIU standards took one of the central axes of the day. The most important officials of the FIU provided the arguments and motivations for the changes in the new legislation.

Patricia Bullrich, Minister of National Security and member of the panel of speakers highlighted the importance of the fight against corruption and drug trafficking as two axes of crimes that precede money laundering. “For us there are two chains that are important: on the one hand, the criminal organization chain, where the distribution, collection, transport and retail distribution chains are located; and on the other, the money route. For us it is essential to disarm all formal and informal money networks in all crimes that precede laundering "

Finally, the director of FAPLA Roberto Bulit Goñi closed the meeting by highlighting the virtuous coincidence between the private and public sectors so that it materializes in the daily evolution. “There is much to be done on both sides, especially the public that is responsible for designing effective criminal policy. For them, recognizing the enormous effort of recent years, we ask them to be open to continue integrating the private sector ”.
